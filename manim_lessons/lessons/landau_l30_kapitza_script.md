# Landau Mechanics 30 — Motion in a rapidly oscillating field: Kapitza's pendulum

> 《Landau–Lifshitz 經典力學》教學系列第 30 課（第五章最後一課）
> 中文標題：快速振盪場中的運動：Kapitza 倒立擺

## 繁體中文旁白

如果粒子除了靜態位能，還受到一個高頻、快速振盪的力，運動就分成兩部分：緩慢的平均運動 X，加上細小的快速抖動 ξ。

快速抖動由高頻力直接決定：m 乘 ξ 的二階導數等於那個力，解出 ξ 等於負 f 除以 m ω²，振幅很小。

把方程對快速振盪取平均，緩慢運動 X 就好像在一個「有效位能」裡運動。

這個有效位能等於原本的位能，再加上一項正比於振盪力振幅平方的修正。

把它用到 Kapitza 擺：讓單擺的支點上下高頻振動，有效位能就多了一項正比於 sin²θ 的能量。

只要振動夠快、夠大，滿足 a²ω² 大於 2gl，擺頂就出現一個新的極小值：倒立的位置竟然變穩定了。

所以快速振盪可以用一個有效位能來描述；正是它，讓 Kapitza 擺能穩穩地倒立在最高點。

## English narration

If a particle feels both a static potential and a fast, high-frequency oscillating force, its motion splits into two parts: a slow average motion X plus a small fast jitter xi.

The fast jitter is fixed directly by the high-frequency force: m times the second derivative of xi equals that force, giving xi equals minus f over m omega-squared, a small amplitude.

Averaging the equation over the fast oscillation, the slow motion X behaves as if it moves in an effective potential.

This effective potential is the original one plus a correction proportional to the squared amplitude of the oscillating force.

Applying this to Kapitza's pendulum, we vibrate the pivot up and down at high frequency, and the effective potential gains a term proportional to sine-squared theta.

If the vibration is fast and strong enough that a-squared omega-squared exceeds two g l, a new minimum appears at the top: the inverted position becomes stable.

So a rapid vibration can be described by an effective potential; that is exactly what lets Kapitza's pendulum balance upside-down at the very top.
