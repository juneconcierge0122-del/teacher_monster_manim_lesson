# Landau Mechanics 03 — Galileo's Principle of Relativity

## 繁體中文旁白

想像一列火車平順地向右行駛。車廂裡的人把球垂直往上丟，月台上的人也正在觀察。對車廂裡的人，球只上下移動；對月台上的人，球同時向右前進。兩個人看到的路徑不同，誰才是正確的？我們先替兩個人各自放上一個座標系。月台的 K 固定不動，車廂的 K prime 以速度 V 等速移動。同一個位置，只是用兩套尺來標記。月台座標 r 等於車廂座標 r prime 加上車廂走過的 V t。在經典力學裡，時間不會因為觀察者改變：t 等於 t prime。對位置公式對時間微分，速度就變成 v 等於 v prime 加上 V。再微分一次，等速移動的 V 沒有加速度，所以兩個座標系測得的加速度完全相同：a 等於 a prime。這就是 Galileo 的相對性原理：所有等速直線運動的觀察者，都能寫出同一套力學定律。

## English narration

Imagine a train gliding smoothly to the right. Someone inside tosses a ball straight up, while an observer on the platform watches. To the passenger, the ball moves only up and down. To the observer on the platform, it also moves to the right. The paths look different. Which observer is correct? Give each observer a coordinate frame. The platform frame K stays fixed, while the train frame K prime moves uniformly with velocity V. They are marking the same position with two different rulers. The platform coordinate r equals the train coordinate r prime plus the distance V t traveled by the train. In classical mechanics, time does not depend on the observer: t equals t prime. Differentiate the position relation once. Velocity becomes v equals v prime plus V. Differentiate again. Because V is constant, it contributes no acceleration. Both observers measure exactly the same acceleration: a equals a prime. This is Galileo’s principle of relativity: every uniformly moving observer can write the same laws of mechanics.
