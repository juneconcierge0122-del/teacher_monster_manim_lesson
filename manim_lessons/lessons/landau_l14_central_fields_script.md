# Landau Mechanics 14 — Central fields: how angular momentum shapes an orbit

> 《Landau–Lifshitz 經典力學》教學系列第 14 課
> 中文標題：中心力場：角動量如何決定軌道

## 繁體中文旁白

當位能只依賴粒子到固定中心的距離 U(r)，力永遠沿半徑方向，這就是中心力場。

角動量 M = r × p 保持不變，所以半徑向量始終位於垂直 M 的平面內，整條軌道必定是平面的。

在極座標中 M = mr²φ̇；因此半徑向量在相等時間掃過相等面積，這正是克卜勒第二定律。

徑向運動可視為一維問題，其有效位能 Ueff(r) = U(r) + M²/(2mr²)；交點 Ueff = E 決定最近與最遠距離。

## English narration

When the potential depends only on distance from a fixed centre, U(r), the force is always radial: this is a central field.

Angular momentum M = r × p is conserved, so the radius vector remains in the plane perpendicular to M and the entire orbit is planar.

In polar coordinates M = mr²φ̇; the radius vector therefore sweeps out equal areas in equal times, which is Kepler's second law.

Radial motion becomes a one-dimensional problem with effective potential Ueff(r) = U(r) + M²/(2mr²); intersections with E set the nearest and farthest distances.
