The goal is to understand degrees of freedom, generalized coordinates, and generalized velocities. The prerequisites are trigonometry, position, and velocity. Do we really need both x and y to describe a pendulum? The two coordinates obey one fixed-length constraint, so they are not independent. One angle determines the entire position. A generalized coordinate is an independent variable that fixes the configuration. We choose q to be the angle theta. Geometry converts the angle into Cartesian coordinates. Differentiate in time: generalized velocity q dot fixes the physical velocity. Think: if you know only q, can you predict which way the bob moves next? No. At the same position it may move left or right; velocity is also needed. A degree of freedom is the number of independent configuration variables. A generalized coordinate need not be Cartesian. Position and velocity together specify the mechanical state.
